When it comes time for a haircut, some men default to wherever is closest — barber or salon. But there are real differences between the two, and choosing the right one matters for the results you get. At The Lab Barbershop in Manor, TX, we believe in the barbershop experience. Here's why.

## The Core Difference

**Barbers** specialize in men's grooming. We're trained in cutting techniques specific to male hair patterns: fades, tapers, line-ups, beard work, and razor shaving.

**Salons** serve a broader clientele and focus on techniques like layering, coloring, blowouts, and chemical treatments. Most salon stylists are trained in cosmetology, which covers a wide range of services.

Neither is "better" in absolute terms — but for men's precision cuts and grooming, a barbershop is the purpose-built choice.

## Key Differences

### Training and Licensing
Barbers complete barbering programs focused specifically on clipper work, razor techniques, and men's facial grooming. Cosmetologists complete broader training that includes chemical services, color theory, and long-hair techniques.

### Tools
Barbers rely heavily on **clippers, trimmers, and straight razors** — tools designed for the short, precise work that men's cuts demand. Salons use more **scissors, thinning shears, and blow dryers**.

### The Experience
Barbershops offer a specific atmosphere. It's social, straightforward, and built around efficiency. You come in, sit down, get a great cut, and leave looking sharp. There's a camaraderie to it.

Salons tend toward a quieter, more appointment-driven model with a wider range of amenities.

### Pricing
Barbershops are typically more affordable for standard men's cuts. A quality fade at a barbershop runs $30–$50, while a men's cut at a salon can range from $40–$80+.

## When to Choose a Barber

Pick a barbershop when you want:
- A **precision fade, taper, or line-up**
- **Beard shaping** and grooming
- A **straight razor** shave or edge-up
- A barber who **cuts men's hair all day, every day**

## When a Salon Might Be Better

Consider a salon when you need:
- **Hair coloring** or highlights
- **Chemical treatments** (perms, relaxers)
- **Longer hairstyles** that need layering
- Services not typically offered at barbershops
