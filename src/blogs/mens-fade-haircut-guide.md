The fade is the most requested haircut in modern barbering — and for good reason. It's clean, versatile, and works on virtually every face shape. But not all fades are created equal. At The Lab Barbershop in Manor, TX, we break down the science behind each type so you can walk in confident about what you want.

## What Is a Fade?

A fade is a haircut technique where hair gradually transitions from short to long. The "fade" refers to the blended gradient between lengths, typically from the sides and back of the head up toward the longer hair on top.

## Types of Fades

### Low Fade
The transition starts just above the ear. This is the most conservative option — ideal for professional environments. It adds structure without being dramatic.

**Best for**: Corporate settings, oval and round face shapes

### Mid Fade
The blend begins at the temple, roughly at eye level. This is the most popular choice at The Lab because it's balanced and universally flattering.

**Best for**: Most face shapes, everyday wear

### High Fade
The transition starts high on the head, near the crown. This is a bold, clean look that creates maximum contrast between the sides and top.

**Best for**: Square and diamond face shapes, statement styles

### Skin Fade (Bald Fade)
The hair fades all the way down to the skin. This is the sharpest, most defined version and requires the most technical skill. It's one of our specialties at The Lab.

**Best for**: Anyone wanting maximum contrast and a crisp look

### Drop Fade
Instead of a straight line around the head, the fade "drops" behind the ear, following the natural curve of the head. This creates a stylish arc effect.

**Best for**: Curly hair, longer styles on top

## How to Choose Your Fade

Consider these three factors:

1. **Your face shape**: Round faces look great with high fades that add height. Square faces work well with mid fades that maintain proportion.
2. **Your lifestyle**: Low fades are lower maintenance. Skin fades need touch-ups every 10–14 days.
3. **Your hair texture**: Curly and coily textures hold fades differently than straight hair. Your barber should adjust the technique accordingly.

## Maintenance Schedule

- **Skin and high fades**: Every 10–14 days
- **Mid fades**: Every 2–3 weeks
- **Low fades**: Every 3–4 weeks
